üåÖ Weekly Routine Strategy

Saturday Morning Low Intensity
  • English L&L Reading: Sticky note quotes for literary devices immediately. Saves Paper 2 prep time.
  • CAS Portfolio: Update reflections weekly. Upload photos now to prevent panic later.
Sunday Morning High Priority
  • Math AA SL (07:30 - 09:00): Do this when fresh.
  • Traffic Light System: Mark questions Red/Amber/Green.
  • Mistake Notebook: Copy 'Red' questions to re-attempt blindly next week.
Sunday Evening Writing Block
  • The Writer's Gym: 19:30 - 20:30. No Reading. Only Writing.
  • Goal: One perfect PETER+L paragraph OR 300 words of "ugly" EE drafting.
Sunday Night Reset
  • Pack Bag: Charge iPad/MacBook.
  • Review Week: What didn't get done? Move to next week's priority.
  • Sleep by 22:30: Non-negotiable.

‚è≥ Time Pocket Optimization

School Free Periods Deep Work
  • Library/Quiet Zone: No phone.
  • Task: Complete the "ugliest" task of the day (e.g., tough Chem problem, drafting definitions).
  • Rule: "If I finish this now, I don't have to do it at home."
Commute (MTR/Bus) Passive Input
  • Flashcards: Anki / Quizlet (Bio definitions, Chem ions).
  • Audio: Listen to English Lit audiobook or Econ podcasts.
  • No Social Media: It drains dopamine before you start working.

üß¨ Biology HL Memorize
  • Active Recall: Don't just re-read notes. Close the book and explain it to a wall.
  • Mark Schemes: IB biology is about keywords. Memorize the exact phrasing from past paper mark schemes.
  • Draw it: Can you draw the Krebs cycle from memory?
üß™ Chemistry HL Practice
  • Stoichiometry: Master the mole concept. It's in everything.
  • Organic Chem: Make a big poster of reaction pathways. Stick it on your bedroom wall.
  • Definitions: 1-2 mark definitions are free points. Don't lose them.
üìâ Economics HL Real World
  • Diagrams: Draw, label, shift. If you can't draw the diagram in 30 seconds, you don't know it.
  • RWE (Real World Examples): Keep a list. "Inflation in Turkey," "Sugar Tax in UK." Link every theory to a story.
  • Evaluation: Always ask "Short run vs Long run" and "Stakeholders."
üìö English L&L SL Analyze
  • Global Issues: Connect every text to a Global Issue (Power, Identity, Culture).
  • Big 5: Audience, Purpose, Content, Theme, Tone. Identify these in the first 2 mins.
  • Quote Bank: maintain a digital bank of versatile quotes for your literary texts.
üß† TOK Slow Burn
  • TOK Journal: See weird news? Ask "How do we know this is true?"
  • Tag with concepts: Bias, Certainty, Truth.
